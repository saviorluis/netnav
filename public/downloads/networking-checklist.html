Networking Event Checklist

Thank you for downloading this checklist! Use this comprehensive guide to prepare for your next networking event and make the most of every opportunity.

Pro Tip: Print this checklist and use it to prepare for each networking event.

Before the Event (1-2 Days Prior)

Research the event and organization

Understand the purpose, format, and who typically attends these events.

Review the attendee list (if available)

Identify key people you want to meet and research their backgrounds.

Set specific networking goals

Example: Meet three new contacts in your industry, find a potential mentor, or learn about job opportunities.

Prepare your elevator pitch

Create a 30-second introduction that clearly explains who you are and what you do.

Prepare thoughtful questions

Have 3-5 open-ended questions ready to start meaningful conversations.

Update your LinkedIn profile

Ensure your profile is current as new connections will likely check it.

Day of the Event

Bring plenty of business cards

Aim for at least 20 cards, even if you don't expect to use them all.

Dress appropriately for the event

Research the dress code or err on the side of professional attire.

Arrive early

This gives you time to acclimate and meet people in a less crowded environment.

Bring a pen and small notebook

Take notes about people you meet and conversation points for follow-up.

Charge your phone

Ensure you can exchange contact information and take photos if needed.

Eat beforehand

This allows you to focus on networking rather than the food.

During the Event

Put your phone away

Be present and engaged with the people around you.

Approach people standing alone

They'll likely appreciate being included in conversation.

Practice active listening

Focus on understanding rather than just waiting for your turn to speak.

Ask for business cards

When you've made a good connection, ask for their card and offer yours.

Take brief notes on business cards

Note conversation topics and potential follow-up items.

Move around the room

Don't spend the entire event with the same people.

After the Event (Within 48 Hours)

Connect on LinkedIn

Send personalized connection requests mentioning where you met.

Send follow-up emails

Reference specific conversation points to jog their memory.

Share relevant resources

If you discussed a specific topic, send an article or resource that might be helpful.

Schedule follow-up meetings

For promising connections, suggest coffee or a virtual meeting.

Add contacts to your networking database

Record key information about each new connection for future reference.

Reflect on the event

Note what went well and what you could improve for next time.
